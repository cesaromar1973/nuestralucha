Here with the late Puerto Rican Revolutionary Rafael Cancel Miranda.
"Miranda, who joined the Puerto Rican Nationalist Party at age 15, was the last surviving member of the four Puerto Rican nationalists and freedom fighters who shocked the world and raised the Puerto Rican flag higher than ever on March 1, 1954. He, alongside the great Lolita Lebron, Irving Flores, and Andres Figueroa Cordero, attacked the chambers of the U.S. Congress to denounce Puerto Rico’s colonial status and to demand independence for the island."(Liberationnews.org)
César Omar Sánchez

Graphic/Web artist and Social Activist. A current student at NJCU pursuing a degree in Philosophy, B.A. César’s past studies are in Computer Science, Media Arts, Web Development, and Nursing. Member of the Honor Society and active in Cuba solidarity work in the New York-New Jersey area.

We dedicate this virtual art show to the following:

Our dear late brother, compañero, and friend…Hugo W. Morales. His Inspiration and Passion for the arts will resonate within us forever.

To all the artists both locally and abroad, keep designing, keep creating, but most importantly keep that same Revolutionary Radical consciousness flow through your artworks.

To all our brothers and sisters in Latin America and the Caribbean going through extraordinary circumstances due to the pandemic and an unfair socio-economic system that continues to oppress us all, KEEP FIGHTING! THE TIME FOR CHANGE IS NOW! We are with you!

Finally, to our brothers and sisters in the Belly of The Beast (United States) and World Wide, we are all in the same boat, striving and fighting hard to build a better world, it’s time to UNITE and DEMAND CHANGE!

Yours truly,

César Omar Sánchez
