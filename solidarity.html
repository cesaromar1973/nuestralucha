Solidarity Messages from organizations supporting this event

The Third Annual LATI Colloquium's Spring Art Exhibit: Nuestra Lucha: Pandemic and Social Decay is a vital contribution to the growing necessary awakening to the struggle against capitalist social decay, intensified and expose more clearly through the coronavirus lens.

Jose Marti wrote:

"Culture, which makes talent shine, is not completely our either, nor can we place it solely at our disposal. Rather, it belongs mainly to our country, which gave it to us, and to humanity from which we receive it as a birthright."

Although Cuba's guarantee of health care and education is well known globally, not as well known, in keeping with Marti's thoughts, culture is regarded as a human right, too.

It is in this spirit, I send solidarity to this important exhibit where we can enjoy and learn from the creators who share their work there. They possess the skill and talent to convey concepts and analysis of contemporary life and struggle in deeply accessible ways. Venceremos!

Cheryl LaBash
co-chair
National Network on Cuba

"The ProLibertad Freedom Campaign is honored to support, promote, and endorse the Third Annual LATI Colloquim this upcoming Spring 2021 Art Exhibiton titled, "Nuestra Lucha."

Artists have always been at the forefront of every social justice movement, revolutionary struggle, and movement for freedom. This Colloquim is giving Latinx artists a much needed opportunity to share their work, perspectives, and opinions in a political climate that is curtailing the right to freedom of speech and dissent.

ProLibertad applauds the efforts of Cesar Omar Sanchez and Alva Toledo to create a safe space where artists can express their anger, criticism, and opinions. We, wholeheartedly, appreciate their efforts to rescue and re-frame many of these incredible Latinx American figures, considered controversial by some, and placing them in their proper political context.

We stand with this colloquim and ask all our allies and supporters to attend, enjoy, ask questions, and learn."

The ProLibertad Freedom Campaign
